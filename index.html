Abstract

In recent years, text-image joint-driven video editing based on diffusion models has achieved great success. With the increasing users' demand for video editing (changing the protagonist or style of the video by inputting images), a single text description for video editing has been unable to meet users' needs. However, none of the current video editing technologies can achieve custom video style editing by inputting images.

In this work, we introduce an innovative general video editing framework named Custom-Style-Editing.

  1. Our core framework is to extract style information by randomly selecting a block of a style image and then fusing it with text information through the BLIP network to obtain the style control signal. Similarly, we can get a reference protagonist control signal. Finally, video editing is accomplished by jointly guiding the diffusion model through style and protagonist control signals.
  2. In addition, we find a conflict between the two signals during the denoising process. When two kinds of signals guide denoising at the same time, one of them cannot be introduced into the generated video. Therefore, we designed a cross-denoising method. During the denoising process, the two signals guide denoising alternately, so the final generated video can contain the changed protagonist and perfectly integrate the style. Our method is comprehensively evaluated on different video data.

Compared with the existing methods, we have more abundant video editing functions, and we have shown better performance in visual consistency quality.
